Spaghetti Carbonara

⏰ 30 Minutes 🍳 Serves 4 Medium Non-Veg
← Back to Recipes

Spaghetti Carbonara is one of Rome's most iconic pasta dishes. Made with just a handful of quality ingredients, the magic lies in the technique: a silky, golden sauce created entirely from eggs, aged cheese, and starchy pasta water, with no cream involved whatsoever. Crispy guanciale adds a savoury depth that makes this dish truly unforgettable.

Ingredients

  • 400 g spaghetti
  • 200 g guanciale or pancetta
  • 4 large egg yolks
  • 1 whole egg
  • 100 g Pecorino Romano, grated
  • 50 g Parmesan, grated
  • 2 tsp freshly ground black pepper
  • Salt for pasta water
  • 1 cup reserved pasta water

Step-by-Step Instructions

1
Bring a large pot of generously salted water to a rolling boil. Add the spaghetti and cook according to the package instructions until just al dente. Before draining, reserve at least 1 cup of the starchy pasta cooking water.
2
While the pasta cooks, slice the guanciale into small cubes or strips. Place them in a cold large skillet and cook over medium heat, stirring occasionally, for 8 to 10 minutes until the fat renders and the pieces turn golden and crispy. Remove the pan from the heat and set aside.
3
In a bowl, whisk together the egg yolks, whole egg, grated Pecorino Romano, and grated Parmesan until combined. Add the freshly ground black pepper generously. Whisk in 2 to 3 tablespoons of the warm pasta water to slightly temper the mixture and loosen it.
4
Drain the pasta and immediately transfer it to the skillet with the guanciale over very low heat. Toss well to combine. Remove the skillet from the heat entirely. Pour the egg and cheese mixture over the hot pasta, tossing vigorously and continuously while adding splashes of the reserved pasta water until a glossy, creamy sauce coats every strand.
5
Serve immediately in warmed pasta bowls. Finish with an extra grating of Pecorino Romano and an additional generous crack of black pepper. Carbonara waits for no one, so eat right away while it is perfectly silky.
