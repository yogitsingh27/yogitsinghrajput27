Spicy Beef Tacos

⏰ 35 Minutes 🍳 Serves 4 Easy Non-Veg
← Back to Recipes

Spicy Beef Tacos are a fiesta of flavour in every bite. Boldly seasoned ground beef cooked with onion, garlic, and a smoky spice blend is piled generously into warm taco shells and topped with vibrant fresh garnishes. Quick to prepare and endlessly customisable, these tacos are perfect for casual dinners, weeknight meals, or feeding a hungry crowd. Set up a topping station and let everyone build their own!

Ingredients

  • 500 g ground beef (minced)
  • 8 to 10 taco shells
  • 1 large onion, finely diced
  • 3 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 tbsp vegetable oil
  • 2 tsp ground cumin
  • 2 tsp smoked paprika
  • 1 tsp chilli powder
  • 1 tsp dried oregano
  • 1 tsp garlic powder
  • 2 tbsp tomato paste
  • 80 ml water or beef stock
  • Salt and black pepper to taste
  • 100 g cheddar cheese, grated
  • 2 tomatoes, diced
  • 1 avocado, sliced
  • Sour cream and lime to serve

Step-by-Step Instructions

1
Prepare all your toppings first so that assembly is quick and easy. Dice the tomatoes, slice the avocado, grate the cheddar, and arrange everything in small bowls. Squeeze lime juice over the avocado to prevent browning. Set all toppings aside and preheat your oven to 180 °C to warm the taco shells later.
2
Heat the vegetable oil in a large frying pan over medium-high heat. Add the diced onion and cook for 5 minutes until softened and starting to turn golden. Add the minced garlic and stir for 1 minute until fragrant.
3
Increase the heat to high and add the ground beef to the pan. Break it apart with a wooden spoon and cook for 6 to 8 minutes, stirring frequently, until the meat is browned all over with no pink remaining. Drain off any excess fat from the pan if needed.
4
Reduce the heat to medium. Add the cumin, smoked paprika, chilli powder, dried oregano, and garlic powder. Stir everything together for 1 minute to toast the spices. Add the tomato paste and water or beef stock, stir well, and simmer for 4 to 5 minutes until the sauce thickens and coats the meat. Season generously with salt and black pepper.
5
Warm the taco shells in the preheated oven for 3 to 4 minutes until heated through and lightly crisp. Spoon the seasoned beef generously into each taco shell. Top with grated cheddar, diced tomatoes, avocado slices, a dollop of sour cream, and a squeeze of fresh lime juice. Serve straight away while everything is warm and fresh.
