Chicken Tikka Masala

⏰ 45 Minutes 🍳 Serves 4 Medium Non-Veg
← Back to Recipes

Chicken Tikka Masala is a vibrant, aromatic dish featuring tender pieces of yoghurt-marinated chicken grilled to perfection and then simmered in a luscious, spiced tomato-cream sauce. Warming spices like cumin, coriander, and garam masala create layers of bold flavour that make this one of the most beloved Indian-inspired dishes worldwide. Best served with fluffy basmati rice or warm naan.

Ingredients

  • 700 g chicken breast, cubed
  • 200 g plain yoghurt
  • 2 tbsp lemon juice
  • 2 tsp garam masala
  • 2 tsp ground cumin
  • 2 tsp ground coriander
  • 1 tsp turmeric
  • 1 tsp chilli powder
  • 2 tbsp vegetable oil
  • 1 large onion, finely diced
  • 4 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 tbsp fresh ginger, grated
  • 400 g crushed tomatoes
  • 150 ml double cream
  • Salt to taste
  • Fresh coriander to garnish

Step-by-Step Instructions

1
Combine the yoghurt, lemon juice, 1 teaspoon garam masala, 1 teaspoon cumin, 1 teaspoon coriander, turmeric, and chilli powder in a large bowl. Add the cubed chicken, mix thoroughly to coat, cover, and marinate in the refrigerator for at least 1 hour or overnight for the best flavour.
2
Thread the marinated chicken onto skewers or spread on a baking tray. Grill under a high broiler or on a preheated grill pan for 10 to 12 minutes, turning halfway, until the chicken is charred at the edges and cooked through. Set aside.
3
Heat the oil in a large, deep pan over medium heat. Add the diced onion and cook for 8 minutes until soft and golden. Add the garlic and ginger and stir-fry for 2 minutes until fragrant. Add the remaining cumin, coriander, and garam masala, stirring for 1 minute to bloom the spices.
4
Pour in the crushed tomatoes and stir well to combine with the spiced onion base. Bring to a gentle simmer and cook for 15 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the sauce thickens and the oil begins to separate on the surface.
5
Stir in the double cream and add the grilled chicken pieces to the sauce. Simmer for a further 5 to 8 minutes until the chicken is heated through and beautifully coated. Taste and adjust seasoning with salt.
6
Serve over steamed basmati rice or alongside warm naan bread. Garnish generously with freshly chopped coriander and a swirl of cream. A squeeze of fresh lemon on top brightens all the flavours beautifully.
