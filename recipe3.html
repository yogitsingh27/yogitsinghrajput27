Chocolate Lava Cake

⏰ 25 Minutes 🍳 Serves 4 Easy Dessert
← Back to Recipes

Chocolate Lava Cake, also known as molten chocolate cake, is the pinnacle of chocolate desserts. A perfectly baked outer shell gives way to a warm, flowing river of liquid chocolate at the centre. Simple to prepare yet breathtakingly impressive, these individual cakes are guaranteed to delight guests and satisfy every chocolate craving with just a handful of pantry staples.

Ingredients

  • 170 g dark chocolate (70%)
  • 115 g unsalted butter
  • 3 large eggs
  • 3 egg yolks
  • 90 g caster sugar
  • 2 tbsp plain flour
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract
  • Pinch of salt
  • Butter and cocoa for moulds
  • Vanilla ice cream to serve
  • Icing sugar to dust

Step-by-Step Instructions

1
Preheat your oven to 220 °C (200 °C fan). Generously butter four individual ramekins or oven-safe cups, then dust each one with cocoa powder, tapping out any excess. This ensures the cakes release cleanly. Place the prepared moulds on a baking tray and set aside.
2
Chop the dark chocolate into small pieces and place in a heatproof bowl along with the unsalted butter. Set the bowl over a saucepan of barely simmering water, ensuring the base does not touch the water. Stir gently until the chocolate and butter are completely melted and smooth. Remove from the heat and allow to cool slightly for 5 minutes.
3
In a separate large bowl, whisk together the eggs, egg yolks, caster sugar, vanilla extract, and pinch of salt. Whisk vigorously for 2 to 3 minutes until the mixture becomes pale, slightly thickened, and creamy.
4
Pour the slightly cooled chocolate mixture into the egg mixture and fold together gently using a spatula until just combined. Sift in the plain flour and fold again until no flour streaks remain. Do not overmix. Divide the batter equally among the four prepared moulds.
5
Bake in the preheated oven for exactly 10 to 12 minutes. The edges should be set and firm while the centre still appears slightly wobbly when gently shaken. Immediately run a knife around the edge of each mould, place a plate on top, and carefully invert. Dust with icing sugar and serve instantly with a scoop of vanilla ice cream.
